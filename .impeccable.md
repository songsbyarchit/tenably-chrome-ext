## Design Context

### Users
Landlords with a small portfolio (2–10 listings) who have imported properties from SpareRoom and need to filter applicants quickly. Desktop-first; mobile app planned but out of scope now. They're time-pressed — they use this between other tasks, not as a dedicated daily tool. The core job: stop wasting time on viewings with tenants who can't afford the rent or haven't submitted documents.

### Brand Personality
**Efficient, focused, practical, smooth.**
Not a slick startup product. More like a well-run letting agency that happens to have great software. Grounded and trustworthy, not flashy.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Default light mode** with a dark mode toggle (persisted in localStorage)
- **Not SpareRoom** (busy, orange, cluttered)
- **A little like Rightmove** (clean, structured, property-portal trust) but **more like Jitty.com** (editorial, generous whitespace, aspirational, magazine-like)
- **Terracotta/warm clay accent** — distinctive in UK proptech (not Rightmove red, not Zoopla purple, not generic teal)
- Off-cream background, white card surfaces, deep navy text
- **Fonts**: Epilogue (headings — focused, geometric) + Manrope (body — humanist, clean, efficient)
- No Supabase-green (#3ecf8e), no glassmorphism, no gradient text

### Design Principles
1. **Scannability first** — landlords need to make decisions fast; layout and hierarchy serve speed
2. **Signal over noise** — every visual element earns its place; no decorative chrome
3. **Grounded trust** — warm neutrals and clean structure convey reliability, not tech-startup energy
4. **Editorial restraint** — generous whitespace, strong type hierarchy, photo-forward listing cards
5. **Consistent readiness signals** — green/amber/red status indicators are the primary language of the interface
